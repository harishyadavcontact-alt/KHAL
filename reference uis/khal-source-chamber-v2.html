Interest rate cycle
Macro / monetary force — slow onset, non-linear consequence
A sustained shift in the rate environment changes the cost of capital, the value of duration, and the fragility of anything financed with variable-rate or short-maturity debt. The force is ergodic in its onset and fat-tailed in its damage to over-leveraged positions.

▾
Variable tranche ($2.4M) reprices at 6.8%. Monthly debt service rises ~$9,600. Runway compresses from 11 to 7 months. Covenant test fails at month 9 under base-case revenue.
→
Swap variable tranche to fixed via rate cap immediately. Trigger 90-day liquidity review. Freeze discretionary capex. Notify board of covenant risk horizon.
Threat
Lender pulls $800K facility at rollover
▾
Primary lender declines renewal. Replacement facility requires 3–4 month lead time. Gap forces draw on operating reserves, exposing cash coverage below 1.2×.
→
Start secondary lender relationship now. Keep facility below 70% utilisation. No surprise draws in 90 days before rollover. Maintain clean covenant record throughout.
Scenario
Rate cut cycle opens refinancing window
▾
Central bank pivots. Fixed-rate window briefly opens at 4.1%. Peers with variable exposure rush simultaneously — spreads compress and lead times lengthen fast.
→
Pre-position: complete lender diligence packages in advance. Move at window open, not at rate bottom. Lock 5-year fixed on primary tranche before spread compression.
