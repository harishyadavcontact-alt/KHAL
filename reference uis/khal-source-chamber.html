Ends — barbell posture
Hedge — downside protected
Eliminate variable-rate exposure above 30% of total debt. Build 6-month cash runway outside operating accounts. No covenant exposure within 2× stress scenario.
Edge — convex upside
Rate normalisation creates refinancing window. Position to lock fixed at peak if rates break. Optionality on distressed acquisition of over-leveraged peers.
Scenario / Threat / Response
Threat
Rates hold above 6% for 18+ months
▾
Variable-rate tranche ($2.4M) reprices at 6.8%. Monthly debt service increases by $9,600. Runway compresses from 11 months to 7. Covenant test fails at month 9 under base case revenue.
→
Immediate: swap variable tranche to fixed via rate cap. Trigger 90-day liquidity review. Freeze discretionary capex. Notify board of covenant risk horizon.
Threat
Rollover cliff — lender pulls $800K facility
▾
Primary lender declines renewal. Replacement facility requires 3–4 month lead time. Gap forces draw on operating reserves, exposing cash coverage ratio below 1.2×.
→
Pre-emptive: begin secondary lender relationship now. Maintain clean covenant record. Keep facility at <70% utilisation. No surprise draws in 90 days before rollover.
Scenario
Rate cut cycle begins Q2 — refinancing window
▾
Central bank pivots. Fixed-rate window opens at 4.1%. Peers with variable exposure rush to refinance simultaneously, compressing spread and lead times.
→
Pre-position: complete lender diligence packages in advance. Move fast when window opens — do not wait for rates to bottom. Lock 5-year fixed on primary tranche.
Response
Barbell execution — runway and optionality
▾
Left side: fix 70% of debt exposure at current rates, build 6-month cash reserve, reduce covenant exposure by refinancing short-dated tranche. Right side: hold 20% of liquidity in short-duration instruments available for distressed acquisition if peer pressure creates opportunity.
Generate — state of affairs

The hedge and edge above justify two downstream branches. An Affair captures the obligation to remove fragility. An Interest captures the option to exploit the rate normalisation window.

→ Affair from Hedge
Eliminate variable-rate exposure and establish 6-month cash reserve. No-ruin sequencing: covenant protection before growth allocation.
→ Interest from Edge
Monitor rate normalisation signal. Pre-position lender docs and distressed peer watch. Expiry: 9 months. Kill: rates stable above 5.5% for 6 months.
Active Craft
Barbell Strategy
Separate exposure into two extremes: maximum safety on the left, convex upside on the right. Nothing in the middle.
Heuristics
—
Never cross a river based on average depth. Simulate worst-case rollover with zero lender cooperation.
—
Do not optimise for expected value in fat-tail environments. Optimise for survival first.
—
If uncertain about rate direction, fix the debt and preserve the optionality in cash.
—
A covenant breach is not a negotiation — it is a transfer of control. Treat it as a cliff.

✕
